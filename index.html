About me

Enrique Hortal
Quesada

Assistant Professor
Department of Advanced Computing Sciences (DACS)
Maastricht University, the Netherlands

Since April 2016, I have been a member of the RAI (Robotics, Agents and Interactions) group at the Department of Advanced Computing Sciences (DACS), Maastricht University. Since August 2019, I work as an assistant professor. My teaching is mainly related to Computer Science and Machine Learning, and since 2019/2020 I coordinate the Bachelor thesis for the BSc in Data Science and Artificial Intelligence.

My background is in brain signal processing and classification as a former member of the Brain Machine Interface Systems Lab at Miguel Hernández University of Elche, Spain, where I obtained a PhD in Industrial and Telecommunications Technologies in February 2016 (Cum Laude).

My research interests span machine/deep learning, human-machine interaction, (bio)signal processing and analysis, and affective computing.

I am a member of the Education Program Committee (EPC) of the department since 2020, and have been coordinating the Cognitive Systems research theme since 2023.

Projects

Research Projects

XR2Learn

Horizon Europe · HORIZON-IA · €7,660,125 · Jan 2023 – Jun 2026

Leveraging the European XR industry technologies to empower immersive learning and training. XR2Learn establishes a cross-border innovation community and Digital Innovation Hub for XR in education, bringing together XR technology providers, application designers, education experts, and end-users. Delivers tools for emotion/affect detection and adaptive learning experiences. Maastricht University is one of 9 consortium partners. Coordinated by CNIT (Italy).

JOINclusion

Erasmus+ · Key Action 2 · €291,240

Fostering the social inclusion of primary school children — particularly those with a migrant background — through a collaborative mobile application. Uses machine learning for affect-based personalisation, promoting empathy, dialogue, and integration.

MaTHiSiS

H2020 · ICT-20-2015 · €7,618,584

Managing Affective-learning THrough Intelligent atoms and Smart InteractionS. An adaptive learning platform for vocational training and mainstream education, supporting individuals with and without intellectual disabilities. Consortium of 18 organisations across 9 EU member states.

BioMot

EU-funded · International consortium

Smart wearable robots with bioinspired sensory-motor skills. Developing cognitive architectures for wearable robotic exoskeletons exploiting neuronal control and learning mechanisms for symbiotic gait behavior in human-robot interaction.

Brain2Motion

National · Hybrid ER-MNP system

A hybrid exoskeletal-neuroprosthesis (ER-MNP) for the upper limb controlled by a multimodal brain-neural computer interface (BNCI). Combines EEG-based BCI and EOG signals for reaching and grasping operations in patients with neurological conditions.
